Featured Artists

Fernando Botero

Oil painting, watercolor, pastel, charcoal drawing and sculpture.

Born on 1932, is known for his unique style of painting and sculpture featuring exaggerated
voluminous forms. His work includes portraits, landscapes, and depictions of everyday life,
as well as political and social themes.

Alejandro Obregón

Oil painting, watercolor, collage, printmaking and sculpture.

Born on 1920, his work often featured bold colors, abstract forms, and a unique blend of European modernism and Colombian culture.

Beatriz Gonzalez

Oil painting, watercolor, drawing, installations and graphic design.

Born on 1938, her art often involves the appropriation of images from mass media and advertising, which she incorporates into her paintings, collages, and installations.

Luis Caballero

Oil paint and charcoal on canvas, paper, and other surfaces.

Born on 1943, Caballero's work challenged traditional notions of masculinity and sexuality in Latin America and drew on influences from European modernism and classical art. His work is characterized by a highly expressive, gestural style that captures the emotional intensity of his subjects.

Santiago Cárdenas

Acrylic paint on canvas, with a highly textured, layered surface.

Born on 1937, he is known for his contributions to the development of abstract art in Colombia and his use of unconventional materials and techniques in his work. Cárdenas's art often featured geometric shapes and bold, contrasting colors arranged in complex, rhythmic patterns that evoked a sense of movement and dynamism.

Ana Mercedes Hoyos

Oil paint, acrylics, textiles, and found objects.

Born on 1942, she is known for her vibrant, colorful paintings, sculptures, and installations that often explored themes related to Colombian culture and identity.
